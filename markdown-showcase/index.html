# Mathematical Concepts ## Linear Algebra The **eigenvalue equation** for a matrix $A$ is: $$A\mathbf{v} = \lambda\mathbf{v}$$ Where $\lambda$ is the eigenvalue and $\mathbf{v}$ is the corresponding eigenvector. ## Calculus The **fundamental theorem of calculus** states: $$\int_a^b f'(x) dx = f(b) - f(a)$$ ## Statistics The **normal distribution** probability density function: $$f(x) = \frac{1}{\sigma\sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{x-\mu}{\sigma}\right)^2}$$ ## Complex Analysis **Euler's formula** connects trigonometry and complex exponentials: $$e^{i\theta} = \cos\theta + i\sin\theta$$ This leads to the famous **Euler's identity**: $$e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0$$
